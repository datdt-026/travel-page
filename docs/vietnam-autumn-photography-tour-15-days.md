# Vietnam Autumn Photography Tour

## 15-Day Custom Photo Expedition

**Brand:** Vietway Travel

**Main Focus:** Landscape photography, ethnic portraiture, rice terraces, waterfalls, ancient architecture, and drone photography.

## Tour Overview

The Vietnam Autumn Photography Tour is a 15-day custom photo expedition through some of Northern and Central Vietnam's most photogenic landscapes and cultural regions.

The journey traverses Hanoi, Bac Son, Cao Bang, Binh Lieu, Mu Cang Chai, Da Nang, and Hoi An. It is designed for photographers seeking golden rice terraces, soft morning mist, limestone valleys, ethnic minority portraiture, waterfalls, traditional craft villages, and atmospheric ancient-town scenes.

The itinerary is carefully timed for autumn, when harvest fields turn gold, waterfalls reach strong seasonal volume, and mountain valleys often carry soft morning fog.

## Autumn Season Highlights

Autumn is the central visual theme of this expedition. The season offers golden harvest fields, soft morning mist, peak waterfall volume, and active farming scenes across the northern highlands.

In Mu Cang Chai, September and October are peak harvest months. The rice terraces shift into gold and amber tones, while local farmers work in the fields from dawn to dusk. This creates strong opportunities for landscape, environmental portrait, and cultural storytelling photography.

## Exclusive Drone Privileges

This tour includes rare drone photography access in several key locations.

### Bac Son and Phong Nam Valley

Na Lay Peak in Bac Son offers exclusive sunrise and sunset drone shooting opportunities over the Bac Son rice plain.

Phong Nam Valley provides scenic mountain corridor drone access, allowing photographers to capture aerial views of limestone peaks, valleys, and rural landscapes.

### Ban Gioc Waterfalls

Ban Gioc Waterfalls is one of the most important exclusive features of this tour. Located on the Vietnam-China border, it is one of Asia's most majestic waterfalls.

Vietway Travel states that it is the only licensed drone operator in Vietnam permitted to fly at Ban Gioc Waterfalls. This gives the tour a distinctive aerial photography advantage that is rarely available to visiting photographers.

## Day-by-Day Itinerary

## Day 1 - Hanoi Arrival

**Theme:** Immersive Introduction to Vietnam's Historic Capital

Upon arrival at Noi Bai International Airport, the guide and driver meet the group and transfer them to the hotel in the Old Quarter or West Lake area.

Key activities:

- Private airport pickup and hotel transfer
- Hotel check-in and rest
- Optional evening stroll around Hoan Kiem Lake for warm-up street photography

Hanoi serves as the base for the night before the expedition heads north into the limestone highlands.

## Day 2 - Hanoi to Bac Son and Quynh Son Village

**Theme:** Dramatic Karst Valleys and Ethnic Cultural Exchange

The group departs Hanoi early and travels north to Lang Son Province, entering one of Vietnam's most dramatic valley landscapes.

Key activities:

- Morning drive from Hanoi to Bac Son, approximately 3 hours
- Photography session at Huu Lien grassland meadows
- Afternoon shoot at Na Lay Peak, also known as Bac Son Viewpoint
- Panoramic views over the Bac Son rice plain
- Drone photography permitted
- Evening cultural exchange at Quynh Son Tay ethnic village
- Hat then folk singing performance
- Overnight in Quynh Son village homestay or Bac Son town

Na Lay Peak is most dramatic at sunrise or late afternoon. Midday light is considered harsh and flat, so the shooting window is planned accordingly.

## Day 3 - Bac Son to Cao Bang via Ha Lang

**Theme:** Highland Road Trip and Rolling Hills Photography

This is a long but rewarding transfer day through the northeastern highlands, with shooting opportunities along the route.

Key activities:

- Early morning photography of Bac Son valley and rice fields at golden hour
- Depart Bac Son around 9:00 AM
- Drive 3 to 4 hours to Ha Lang district, Cao Bang
- Afternoon photography at Ba Quang Mound
- Rolling hills and ethnic village scenery
- Late afternoon transfer to Phong Nam Valley
- Check-in and rest

Ba Quang Mound offers excellent mid-afternoon sidelight for landscape portraits and wide-angle compositions.

## Day 4 - Phong Nam, God's Eye Mountain, and Ban Gioc

**Theme:** Misty Valley Peaks and Majestic Geological Wonders

This is one of the most visually powerful days of the tour, moving from misty mountain valleys to one of Southeast Asia's most spectacular waterfalls.

Key activities:

- Early morning photography at Phong Nam Valley
- Mist rising over rice terraces and limestone peaks
- Mid-morning transfer to God's Eye Mountain, also known as Nui Mat Than
- Photography of circular rock formations and geological scenery
- Afternoon arrival at Ban Gioc Waterfalls
- Drone shooting at Ban Gioc with an exclusive local photographer partner
- Optional visit to Nguom Ngao Cave if time permits

Ban Gioc is best photographed from mid-morning to mid-afternoon when light hits the cascade directly. The specialist partner manages the drone permit and on-site operation.

## Day 5 - Ngoc Con Fields and Ban Gioc Waterfalls

**Theme:** Multi-Angle Landscape Session and Immersive Nature Walks

This day is fully dedicated to the Ban Gioc area, maximizing time at one of the tour's most important landscape locations.

Key activities:

- Morning photography at Ngoc Con rice fields
- Autumn paddy terraces and golden field textures
- Extended shoot at Ban Gioc Waterfalls from 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM
- Multiple angles, light conditions, and river mist compositions
- Rest of day at leisure or short nature walk around the valley

The combination of Ngoc Con fields and Ban Gioc Waterfalls creates two very different subjects within a short driving radius.

## Day 6 - Cao Bang to Binh Lieu

**Theme:** Highland Border Journey and Dao Ethnic Culture Preview

After the extra Cao Bang shooting day, the route turns west toward Quang Ninh Province.

Key activities:

- Optional final sunrise shoot at Ban Gioc or Ba Quang
- Direct transfer to Binh Lieu, Quang Ninh Province
- Drive time approximately 5 to 6 hours
- Check-in at Song Mooc House
- Evening scout around Dao Thanh Phan village surroundings

Song Mooc House has experience coordinating portrait sessions with local Dao women and assists with scheduling and community introductions.

## Day 7 - Binh Lieu Dao Thanh Phan Portrait Session

**Theme:** Ethnic Portraiture and Highland Daily Routines

Binh Lieu is home to the Dao Thanh Phan community, known for visually distinctive traditional attire and red box-shaped headdresses.

Key activities:

- Morning portrait photography session with Dao Thanh Phan women
- Traditional attire and red angular headdress
- Afternoon village life photography
- Daily routines, textile work, children, and community scenes
- Optional walk to nearby highland viewpoints

Portrait sessions are coordinated with community consent. Photographers are expected to follow guide instructions regarding boundaries and respectful engagement.

## Day 8 - Binh Lieu to Hanoi or Long Coc

**Theme:** Flexible Return Route and Custom Routing Options

This is a flexible routing day with two possible options depending on the group's interests.

### Option A: Standard Route

- Transfer from Binh Lieu to Hanoi
- Drive time approximately 4 to 5 hours
- Rest afternoon in Hanoi

### Option B: Recommended Route

- Transfer to Long Coc tea hill in Phu Tho
- Rolling green hills suitable for drone and landscape photography
- Overnight at a local guesthouse
- Continue to Mu Cang Chai the next morning with more daylight on arrival

Long Coc adds visual variety and avoids a late arrival in Mu Cang Chai.

## Day 9 - Hanoi to Mu Cang Chai

**Theme:** Epic Northwest Mountain Drive and Panoramic Highland Views

This is an iconic mountain drive through the northwest corridor to one of Vietnam's most celebrated rice terrace landscapes.

Key activities:

- Early morning departure from Hanoi
- Total drive time approximately 5 to 6 hours
- Photography stop at Tu Le Valley
- Bowl-shaped terraces and Khang minority village
- Photography at Khau Pha Pass
- Panoramic highland views from one of Vietnam's great mountain passes
- Arrival and check-in at Mu Cang Chai

## Day 10 - Mu Cang Chai Free Shooting Day

**Theme:** Full-Day Unstructured Photography and Immersive Culture

This is a flexible, unstructured photography day in Mu Cang Chai. The guide suggests locations based on light, weather, and harvest activity.

Key activities:

- Sunrise shoot at La Pan Tan or Ze Xu Phinh terraces
- Mid-morning photography in De Xu Phinh commune
- Terraces at multiple elevations
- Leading-line compositions
- Afternoon village market or Mong minority village visit
- Sunset from a high ridge point

Mu Cang Chai's terrace light changes dramatically throughout the day, so the schedule remains flexible around golden-hour opportunities.

## Day 11 - Mu Cang Chai to Hanoi, Da Nang, and Hoi An

**Theme:** Connecting Mountains to Heritage Coastlines

This travel day connects the mountainous north to the historic coastline of Central Vietnam.

Key activities:

- Final sunrise shoot at Mu Cang Chai
- Transfer to Hanoi, approximately 5 to 6 hours
- Flight from Hanoi to Da Nang
- Transfer from Da Nang Airport to Hoi An, approximately 45 minutes
- Check-in and evening rest
- Optional evening stroll through Hoi An Ancient Town's lantern-lit streets

## Day 12 - Hoi An

**Theme:** Living Craft Traditions and Waterway Photography

This day focuses on waterway traditions, basket boats, carpentry, and fishing net weaving.

Key activities:

- Early morning transfer to Cam Thanh village
- Photography of traditional round basket boats among nipa palm waterways
- Best light from 6:00 to 8:30 AM
- Kim Bong carpentry and net-weaving quarter
- Photography of local artisans at work
- Afternoon transfer to Tra Nhieu village
- Women weaving blue fishing nets along the Thu Bon River estuary
- Optional cast net session for action portrait shots

Special concepts:

- Exclusive portrait session with a master elderly basket boat artisan
- Close-up studies of weathered hands and bamboo patterns
- Traditional shipbuilding photography in active wooden shipyards
- Industrial-heritage compositions with vessel skeletons, timber textures, and workshop tools

## Day 13 - Hoi An

**Theme:** Pre-Dawn Coastal Portraits and Tam Tien Fish Market

This is a high-impact photography day that combines coastal portraiture, ancient-town light, and working fish market scenes.

Key activities:

- Pre-dawn portrait session with fisherman Do Van Muoi casting nets
- Early morning photography in Hoi An Ancient Town
- Lanterns, yellow facades, and quiet alleyways before tourist crowds arrive
- Morning visit to Tam Tien fish market
- Working fish auction market south of Hoi An town

Tam Tien Market is weather-sensitive from September onward because typhoon season may reduce fishing activity. The visit should be confirmed with local contacts 1 to 2 days in advance.

## Day 14 - Hoi An

**Theme:** Intimate Portrait Sessions and the Soul of the Ancient Town

This quieter day focuses on intimate portraiture and free exploration in Hoi An.

Key activities:

- Morning portrait session with Bui Thi Xong
- Elderly local woman and beloved portrait subject among photographers
- Afternoon free time
- Explore Hoi An Ancient Town
- Cafe time, rest, shopping, or independent photography

## Day 15 - Hoi An to Da Nang, Hanoi, and Departure

**Theme:** Leisurely Morning in the Ancient Town and Farewell Vietnam

The final day allows for a relaxed close to the expedition before departure.

Key activities:

- Free morning in Hoi An
- Leisurely breakfast
- Final stroll through the Ancient Town
- Shopping or rest
- Transfer to Da Nang International Airport
- Evening flight from Da Nang, connecting via Hanoi or other transit hubs to international destinations
